🎯 Main Idea & Key Details

What the Text Is Mostly About

The main idea is what a passage is mostly about. Key details are the facts and examples that support and explain it. 🎯

📚 Read this passage:

Built for the Hunt
Eagles are amazing hunters. Their eyes are eight times sharper than a human's, so they can spot prey from far away. Their sharp talons grip tightly. Their hooked beak tears food easily. Every part of an eagle helps it hunt.

🧭 Main Idea vs Details

Main idea: Eagles are built to be great hunters.
Key details: sharp eyes, strong talons, hooked beak.

⚖️ Compare & Contrast

Two Texts, Side by Side

To compare means to tell how two things are alike. To contrast means to tell how they are different. Good readers do both with two texts. ⚖️

📚 Two short texts:

Text A: Wolves hunt in packs. Working as a team, they can take down prey much larger than themselves.

Text B: Tigers hunt alone. A tiger uses stealth and a powerful pounce to surprise its prey.

⚖️ Alike and Different

Alike (compare)Both are predators that hunt prey
Different (contrast)Wolves hunt in packs; tigers hunt alone
Signal words: alikeboth, also, similarly, same
Signal words: differentbut, however, unlike, while

🧱 Prefixes, Suffixes & Roots

Building & Breaking Words

Words have parts. A root is the base. A prefix goes in front and changes meaning. A suffix goes at the end. Knowing word parts helps you unlock new words! 🧱

🧩 Word Building Blocks

Prefixfront part: re- (again), un- (not), pre- (before)
Root / Basethe main word: play, view, happy
Suffixend part: -ful (full of), -less (without), -ed
Exampleun + happy = unhappy (not happy)

🔍 Decode It

re + view = review — to view again.   fear + less = fearless — without fear.

🏗️ Text Structure

How a Text Is Organized

Authors organize nonfiction in text structures. Spotting the structure helps you understand and remember what you read. 🏗️

📐 Four Common Structures

Sequenceevents or steps in order
Cause & Effectwhy something happens and the result
Compare & Contrasthow things are alike and different
Problem & Solutiona problem and how it is solved

🐊 Spot the Structure

"Because the river dried up, the crocodiles moved downstream." This tells why something happened and the result — that is cause and effect.

✍️ RACE Response

Writing a Strong Text Answer

RACE is a plan for answering a reading question in writing: Restate, Answer, Cite evidence, Explain. ✍️

🏁 The RACE Steps

R — RestateTurn the question into a sentence
A — AnswerGive your clear answer
C — CiteUse proof from the text
E — ExplainTell how the proof supports your answer

📝 Example

Question: Why do wolves hunt in packs?
RACE answer: Wolves hunt in packs (R) so they can catch larger prey (A). The text says working as a team lets them take down bigger animals (C). This shows teamwork makes the pack stronger hunters (E).
