🛰II · Factor Finder

A factor is a number that divides EVENLY into another number (no remainder). Factors come in pairs that multiply to the target. Example: factors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12 — because 1×12, 2×6, 3×4 all equal 12.

⭐IV · Prime Scanner

A PRIME number has exactly TWO factors: 1 and itself. A COMPOSITE number has MORE than two factors. Special case: 1 is neither prime nor composite. The smallest prime is 2 — and it’s the only EVEN prime!
PRIME vs COMPOSITE 7 is PRIME — only 1 × 7 9 is COMPOSITE — 1 × 9 AND 3 × 3
