➗ Long Division

Division with Remainders

Long division means sharing a number into equal groups. Sometimes there's a leftover — that's the remainder. Use the steps: Divide → Multiply → Subtract → Bring down. 🔄

📚 Walk through 158 ÷ 6

Step 1 — Divide: 15 ÷ 6 = 2 (since 6×2 = 12). Write 2 above the 5.

Step 2 — Multiply & Subtract: 15 − 12 = 3.

Step 3 — Bring down the 8 to make 38.

Step 4 — Divide again: 38 ÷ 6 = 6 (since 6×6 = 36). 38 − 36 = 2 left over.

✨ 158 ÷ 6 = 26 R2

🔍 Factors & Multiples

Prime & Composite Numbers

A factor divides a number evenly. A multiple is what you get when you skip-count. A prime number has exactly two factors; a composite has more. 🧩

🧮 Factors vs. Multiples

Factors of 121, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12 — they divide 12 evenly
Multiples of 1212, 24, 36, 48 — skip-counting by 12
Primeexactly 2 factors: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13
Compositemore than 2 factors: 4, 6, 8, 9, 10

⚖️ Equivalent Fractions

Same Value, Different Look

Equivalent fractions name the same amount. Multiply OR divide the top and bottom by the SAME number to find them. 🔄

🔢 Building Equivalent Fractions

Multiply both parts: 1/2 = (1×3)/(2×3) = 3/6. Same amount of pizza!

Simplify by dividing: 6/8 = (6÷2)/(8÷2) = 3/4. Always divide by the largest factor they share.

1/2= 2/4 = 3/6 = 5/10
1/3= 2/6 = 3/9 = 4/12
2/3= 4/6 = 6/9 = 8/12
Simplest formwhen 1 is the only shared factor

🔢 Comparing Fractions

Which Fraction Is Greater?

To compare fractions: if denominators match, compare the numerators. If not, find a common denominator first — or use the benchmark 1/2. 🧭

🧭 Three Comparing Tricks

Same bottom3/8 < 5/8 — just compare tops
Same top1/4 < 1/3 — fewer pieces = bigger pieces
Common denominator2/3 vs 3/4 → 8/12 vs 9/12 → 3/4 wins
Benchmarkcompare each to 1/2

🧠 Word Problems

Multi-Step Safari Problems

Multi-step problems need a plan. Read carefully, decide each step, solve in order, and check that your answer makes sense. 🗺️

🐘 Worked Example

A safari park has 6 elephants. Each eats 45 kg of hay daily, plus the park adds 30 kg of fruit total. How much food per day?

Step 1: Hay = 6 × 45 = 270 kg.

Step 2: Add fruit = 270 + 30 = 300 kg per day.
