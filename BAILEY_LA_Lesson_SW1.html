🐘 The Watering Hole 🌍

The dry season had stretched on for many weeks, and the savanna was parched — the ground was cracked and thirsty. At the old watering hole, Tamu the young elephant flicked her tail at a buzzing fly and stared at the mud. Water had become scarce. There was barely enough left for the birds.

"We must walk to the great river," announced Nia, the herd's wise grandmother. "There, water is always plentiful."

Tamu was reluctant — she did not want to leave the only home she knew. But a young elephant cannot argue with a thirsty herd. At sunrise, the elephants ventured across the golden grass, traveling for two days past sleeping lions and towering giraffes.

At last, Tamu heard it: the rush of cool water. The great river! She charged down the bank and splashed in, trumpeting with joy. The herd drank until the stars came out.

Walking home weeks later, when the rains had returned, Tamu felt triumphant, like a hero coming back from a winning adventure. She had learned that when the herd sticks together, no journey is too far.

Feed any story into the machine and a perfect summary comes out:

WWanted
(their goal)
BBut
(the problem)
SSo
(what they did)

(how it ended)

Remember: tiny details — like the buzzing fly — never make it into the machine!

Reader Skill · 10 points

🔍 Word Detective: Context Clues

When a word is a mystery, the author usually hides the key nearby. Detective moves:

  • Dash or comma clue: the meaning sits right after it. "parched — the ground was cracked and thirsty"
  • Sentence clue: the next sentence explains. "Water had become scarce. There was barely enough left for the birds."
  • Feeling clue: what's happening tells you the emotion.
