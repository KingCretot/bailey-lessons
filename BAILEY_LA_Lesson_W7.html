🎯 Theme & Central Message

The Big Lesson

The theme is the lesson or message a story teaches — like "never give up" or "kindness matters." It is not stated directly; the reader figures it out from what happens. 🎯

📚 Read this short tale:

The Wolf and the Long Winter
Young Willa the wolf wanted to hunt alone to prove she was strong. For days she found nothing and grew cold and hungry. When the pack invited her back, she finally understood: the pack survived the winter because they worked together. From then on, Willa hunted with her family.

🧭 Finding the Theme

Willa learns that teamwork beats going it alone. The theme is: we are stronger when we work together.

👁️ Point of View

Who Is Telling the Story

Point of view tells us who is narrating. First person uses I, me, we — a character tells it. Third person uses he, she, they — an outside narrator tells it. 👁️

🗺️ Two Points of View

First PersonUses I, me, my, we — a character speaks
Third PersonUses he, she, they — an outside narrator
First exampleI crept past the sleeping lion.
Third exampleShe crept past the sleeping lion.

🌈 Figurative Language

Similes & Metaphors

Figurative language paints pictures with words. A simile compares using like or as. A metaphor says one thing IS another. 🌈

🎨 Two Comparison Tools

MetaphorSays one thing IS another
Simile exampleThe cheetah ran like the wind.
Metaphor exampleThe desert was an oven at noon.

📝 Summarizing

Telling the Most Important Parts

A summary retells only the most important parts of a text in a few sentences — the main events, in order, without tiny details. 📝

📚 Read this passage:

The Great Migration
Each year, huge herds of zebra and wildebeest travel across the African plains. They follow the rain to find fresh grass. The journey is dangerous — they must cross rivers full of crocodiles. Still, millions of animals make the trip, and it is one of nature's greatest events.

✅ A Good Summary

"Every year, huge herds cross the African plains following the rain. They face dangers like crocodile-filled rivers, but millions complete the journey."

Notice: it keeps the main idea and leaves out small details.

📰 Text Features

Tools That Help Readers

Text features are parts of nonfiction that help readers find and understand information — headings, captions, bold words, diagrams, and the glossary. 📰

🔍 Common Text Features

HeadingTells what a section is about
CaptionExplains a photo or picture
Bold wordA key vocabulary term to notice
GlossaryLists word meanings at the back

🦅 Why They Matter

If you want to learn what an eagle eats, a heading like "What Eagles Eat" sends you straight to the right section — no need to read the whole book!
